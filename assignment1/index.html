First Impression

1. The first thing that grab my attention?

The first thing I paid attention to was the text following my cursor, writing "click anywhere, to enable sound", followed by a text on the bottom that I scanned briefly telling me to enable the music, so I did. Then, the classic music that played after I enabled the music took my full attention, it’s the first action the website told the users to do, and it marks the beginning of the interactive journey. Moreover, the unidentified sketches on the background appear with a gigantic title text on the bottom. The music plays a crucial role in setting the mood for the user. Additionally, the vibrant orange as the background color also took my attention, it's giving off the vibe that this website is friendly and welcoming.

2. In the first two minutes, what actions did I do?

Landing Page

  • Enabling music - Users are given the option to activate the music by clicking anywhere on the web, the action cue is shown through the small text in the cursor
  • Playing with cursor - each time we hover the cursor, a word appears and follows the cursor as it moves around and eventually forms a sentence that is quite hard to read, which I assume is not intended to be read, it's merely for users to play around with
  • Title sizes change - each of the title rows shrinks or expands as we move the cursor around the area. The website effectively uses existing assets and turns them into something interesting that's related to the theme. The shrinking text experience reminds me of turning the volume up or down, moreover, the title somewhat looks like an audio waveform.

Video page (Introduction to the Concert Hall)

  • As we scroll down, a video of the concert hall begins to play, pausing when we stop scrolling. This video is essentially a series of images that form a video or moving images as we scroll. The video showcases the building from various angles, with brief explanations on the corner of the page. This dynamic presentation keeps users engaged, as our attention span is short. By continuously changing the video angles and providing new information, users remain interested each time a new perspective is revealed.
  • Reading text - While the video is playing, a short explanation text appears in the corner of the page. The text is written concisely, with one long sentence in 3 to 4 short rows.

Interview Page (Music change)

  • Listening to the interview - it's separated to six short interview sections, where users attention is still mantained with the subtitle on the bottom right corner

Reading the explanation. They did a good job of conveying boring information in an interactive way, making learning about the concert hall a very enjoyable journey. Moreover, the text is made simplified explaining only the important bit. Those texts are located in the corner of the screen. This requires me some time because even though it's short, I still have to READ it (not SCAN). I was forced to read it because it tells me that it's important information, and that's the purpose of this web, if I missed it I'll essentially miss the whole point of opening this website. Though the reading time doesn't take that long, it is still the longest activity that I have done on this website

5. What's my impression on the primary goal?

Their primary goal is to introduce the Walt Disney Concert Hall by Frank Gehry through an immersive experience, and they have successfully achieved that. Introducing designer architecture through a website is a brilliant concept, functioning like a digital museum that allows anyone to visit anytime. The website's interactivity is easy to understand and navigate, which can amaze first-time visitors. In other words, they’ve succeeded in creating minimalist interactions that have a significant impact on users. Moreover, the interaction within the website mirrors the experience users would have if they were in the Concert Hall in person. Overall, I am amazed at how brilliantly this website has been crafted to achieve its goal.

Critical Analysis

6. The How does the interactivity communicate the goal?

The well-designed interactions not only create a meaningful journey but also allow users to experience a bit of the high-quality building design through the high-quality website design. This means the interaction within the website brilliantly communicates the long design process of the concert hall, which has gone through many iterations. The users are brought to see the journey through the concert hall tour, interview with the architect, and even the history of the design competition back in the 19s. Through this, users have the full experience of visiting the concert hall, learning every detail about it, therefore this online interaction can compete with the offline interaction visitors can get if they go to the hall physically.

7. How the design encourages ongoing interaction?

The website requires continual interaction where user’s action is mostly required, action like scrolling or pressing on secondary button. Initially if the user’s only scan the text and watch the full interview video, then continually scroll, it’ll take up to 5 minutes to reach the last slide of the page. More time is required if the all text is being read, it could be up to 10-15 minutes depending on the reading speed of a person. With only simple action like scrolling down, there’re big chance user’s will scroll down until the end and access all the information if compare to moving to different pages that usually requires some thinking. With the music and videos, the interactivity is even made smoother, making users journey doesn’t feel that long. Therefore, my impression on the interaction

8. Does the website provides features that encourage users to engage with it repeatedly?

As mentioned before, the website is created to introduce visitors about the concert hall designed by Frank Gehry, where the website can work as a brand identity of the hall by creating an online presence in the online world. Thus this website can be use for a long time, where every time new users want to find information regarding the hall, they’ll come to this website. However, after some time visit and gaining the knowledge about the hall, they’ll less likely to comeback. Unless, they want to further analyse the website interactivity by playing through it, then it’ll require some times visiting back. The website itself considered as an informational content, where it displays facts and history, however it can also work as a promotional content, to influence audience to visit the concert hall, with the usage of multimedia content like images, video and even music. Other than that, there is no unique features the website could offer that requires repeated visit.

Website Reference

6. What other media forms does the experience reference?

  • Physical gallery. Each slide on the website is like a room in a gallery, it represents different information with different background music, just as we enter a new room with different wall colors, room sizes, and decorations.
  • Digital Video. In my opinion, if the whole experience is simplified, we can access it as a video format, because scrolling down to continue watching the video and stop scrolling to stop the video has a similar mental model to watching the video and pressing the pause button to stop. What’s different is the interaction within the website, where the user can easily go to different areas and repeat the interaction many times, like shrinking the title page, that kind of interaction can’t be done in a video format where the interaction is only one way.
  • Common Website. Just like the other website, this website still has the essential structure a website could have, like headings, text, images, and most importantly buttons.
